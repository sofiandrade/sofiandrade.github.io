<h1> About Sofia <h1>

<p>
Born and raised in Miami, FL, Sofia Andrade is the daughter of Ecuadorian immigrants and a first-generation college student at Harvard University, where she is pursuing a joint concentration in History & Literature and Art, Film, & Visual Studies. 
<p>

<p>
She is also a journalist, writing most about music, art, culture, and politics. Through her work, she has covered a broad range of topics — from anti-Blackness in Miami's Latinx communities to hypermasculinity in young Republicans, and from sexual assault in the indie music scene to the complexities of Cuban American political identity. 
<p>
  
<p>
Most recently, she worked as a reporter with WhoWhatWhy, where she covered Florida politics and election integrity leading up to the 2020 election. She also serves as a staff writer in multiple publications, including the Harvard Crimson, the Harvard Political Review, and Pure Nowhere Magazine. 
</p>
